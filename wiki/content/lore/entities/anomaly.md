### ▸ TYPE

Phenomenological Construct — Unbound

### ▸ CHANCE TO ENCOUNTER

Relic

### ▸ DEFINITION

An Anomaly is a disruption in the fundamental principles of known reality.
It may or may not possess sentience or form, but its presence causes consistent violations of physical, cognitive, temporal, or ontological laws.

### ▸ CHARACTERISTICS

- Often location-bound or zone-propagating
- May not be observable by standard sensory input
- Can cause cascading effects on nearby entities
- Stability varies by class and context

### ▸ SUBTYPES

- Temporal Anomaly
- Spatial Anomaly
- Cognitive / Memetic Anomaly
- Ontological Anomaly
- Biological or Hybrid Anomalies

### ▸ RISK LEVEL

**Class I–VI (Varies)**
Risk depends on volatility, spread potential, and interaction with other phenomena.

### ▸ NOTES

Not all anomalies are entities. Not all can be studied.
Some simply exist — and we adapt.
