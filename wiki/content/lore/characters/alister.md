### ▸ NAME

Alister Thorn

### ▸ AGE

62 years old (as of 2017)

### ▸ ORIGIN

Born in Birmingham, England.
Educated at multiple top-tier European institutions specializing in theoretical physics, cognitive sciences, and anomalous research.

### ▸ AFFILIATION

Co-founder and strategic leader of the Shadow Fellowship (Tínulegur Félagsskapur), a covert scientific organization dedicated to researching and preserving knowledge of the Bleverse anomalies.

### ▸ PROFILE

Alister Thorn is recognized as one of the most brilliant minds in modern anomaly science, blending rigorous methodology with an unorthodox philosophical curiosity.
His work focuses on the intersection of cognitive perception, ontological stability, and the emergent properties of Bleverse-related phenomena.

A natural leader, yet deeply introspective and burdened by existential concerns, Alister walks a fragile line between rational mastery and emotional exhaustion.
His passion for knowledge sometimes eclipses his sense of caution, exposing himself — and others — to profound risks.

### ▸ STRENGTHS

- Exceptional theoretical and applied intelligence
- Strategic foresight under extreme conditions
- Deep loyalty to scientific integrity and trusted allies
- Capacity for rapid adaptation to anomalous events

### ▸ WEAKNESSES

- Prone to obsessive focus at the expense of emotional balance
- Tendency toward isolation during stress periods
- Underlying fear of mortality and insignificance
- Subtle cognitive degradation due to prolonged anomaly exposure

### ▸ CURRENT STATUS (as of 2020)

Operational leadership within Shadow Fellowship operations, overseeing the early stages of Bleverse City initiatives.
Subject to cognitive and physical degradation monitoring following multiple high-intensity anomaly retrieval missions.

### ▸ NOTES

Though viewed by many as an unwavering beacon of reason within chaos, confidential assessments suggest Alister may harbor internal fractures yet unseen by his closest allies.
As the pressure mounts and time runs short, it remains unclear whether he will break — or transcend — the fate awaiting him.
