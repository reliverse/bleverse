### ▸ TYPE

Spatial Anomaly

### ▸ BEHAVIOR

Disrupts geometric consistency, topological coherence, and spatial orientation. Alters the shape, scale, or continuity of physical environments.

### ▸ EFFECTS

- Non-Euclidean architecture (impossible angles, shifting hallways)
- Recursive or looping spaces (infinite corridors, mirrored rooms)
- Directional collapse (left leads to right, up leads to down)
- Fractal expansions (spaces that grow as you observe them)
- Zone compression or dilation (rooms larger inside than outside)
- Location bleeding (multiple places occupying the same coordinates)

### ▸ RISK LEVEL

**Class IV – Dimensional Breach Potential**
May lead to irreversible loss of orientation or spatial entrapment.
Containment requires stabilized anchor points and inertial relocking.

### ▸ SUMMARY

A Spatial Anomaly fractures the sense of place.
It bends geometry, breaks direction, and rewrites the boundaries of containment.
Those who enter may never find the same way out — or discover that the inside was always outside to begin with.
