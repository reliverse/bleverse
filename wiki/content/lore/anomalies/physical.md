### ▸ TYPE

Physical Anomaly

### ▸ BEHAVIOR

Violates fundamental laws of physics. Alters interactions between matter, energy, motion, and environmental constants.

### ▸ EFFECTS

- Gravity inversion or loss (localized zero-G or fall-up events)
- Thermodynamic anomalies (e.g. cold fire, heat-absorbing matter)
- Phase instability (objects partially embedded in solid matter)
- Infinite momentum / frictionless surfaces
- Light refraction into non-visual spectra
- Density shifts (solid objects becoming intangible or collapsing inward)

### ▸ RISK LEVEL

**Class V – Structural Reality Hazard**
Poses significant threat to local spatial integrity.
Containment requires reinforced quantum stabilization protocols.

### ▸ SUMMARY

A Physical Anomaly disrupts the mechanics of reality itself.
Objects may behave contrary to known laws — falling upward, melting without heat, or becoming impossible to touch or contain.
In affected zones, reality is not merely distorted — it is redefined.
