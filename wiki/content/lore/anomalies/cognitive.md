### ▸ TYPE

Cognitive Anomaly

### ▸ BEHAVIOR

Targets perception, interpretation, and conscious thought. Alters the way sentient beings observe, remember, and assign meaning.

### ▸ EFFECTS

**General effects**:

- Memetic distortion (thoughts or concepts that self-propagate)
- Perceptual masking (entities become unnoticeable despite presence)
- Semantic collapse (words lose or shift meaning unpredictably)
- Induced déjà vu, jamais vu, or temporal dissonance
- Thought recursion or idea loops
- Involuntary memory rewriting

**Ambivalence Field effects**:

- **Ambivalent bifurcation**: (simultaneous presence of conflicting emotions, beliefs, or desires, often leading to paralysis of decision-making)
- Affective ambivalence: simultaneous love/hate responses to a single object or person
- Volitional paralysis: inability to choose between opposing actions (ambitendency)
- Intellectual conflict: coexistence of logically incompatible beliefs without resolution
- Chronic uncertainty and recursive self-doubt
- Temporary suppression of moral or ethical bias

**Dissonant Construct effects**:

- **Dissonant Construct**: Induces a persistent cognitive conflict by simultaneously enforcing and undermining incompatible beliefs, memories, or identities within the subject.
- Logical contradiction awareness without resolution
- Identity fragmentation: coexisting behavioral patterns that cannot reconcile
- Self-deception feedback loops
- Moral or ideological dissonance
- Delayed cognitive collapse after prolonged exposure
- Emotional displacement (guilt without cause, anger without direction)

### ▸ RISK LEVEL

**Class III – Psychocognitive Contagion**
Anomaly may spread via information alone.
Containment requires cognitive firewalling and limited personnel exposure.
Though not always externally destructive, long-term exposure causes identity erosion, psychogenic fatigue, or value system collapse.

**The Ambivalence Field** doesn't destroy the mind — it splits it.
Victims remain functional, yet unable to determine right from wrong, love from fear, truth from contradiction.
In Blefelheim, it is said this anomaly does not speak. It **asks**. And it never answers.

**Dissonant Construct**: Subjects often appear stable until forced to act on conflicting cognitions. Collapse may be delayed, but inevitable.

### ▸ SUMMARY

A Cognitive Anomaly hijacks the mind.
It reshapes not what is — but what _is perceived to be_.
Subjects may forget truths, believe impossibilities, or fail to notice their own thoughts turning against them.
Exposure does not always feel like exposure.

**Ambivalence Field effects**:

It may induce **ambivalent states** where opposing feelings coexist, rendering the subject emotionally and logically inert.
What follows is not chaos — but a frozen war between certainty and its mirror.
Detected near Zones where Deity-Class echoes have been registered. Believed to be residual effect or emergent side-reaction.

**Dissonant Construct effects**:

This anomaly creates **internal contradiction as an existential structure**.
Victims are not confused — they are **over-aware**. They simultaneously believe and disbelieve.
They act while denying action, justify harm while embracing virtue.
Their minds split not into multiple personalities — but into unresolved truths.
First observed in proximity to Blefelheim-linked constructs.
Correlation suspected between exposure and ideological drift in long-term operatives.
Containment protocols must include external validation agents. Subjects often report "emotional noise" or "value echoes."
