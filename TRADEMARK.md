# Trademark Policy

This document explains how the **Bleverse™** name and branding may be used.

> **Notes:**
> - Open-source licenses grant rights to use, modify, and redistribute **code**.
> - They do **not** automatically grant rights to use **names, logos, or branding**.
> - This policy describes the project's claimed brand identifiers and aims to prevent user confusion.
> - Bleverse™ is an unregistered trademark of Nazarii Korniienko.
> - Bleverse may be registered as a trademark in the future.
> - This document is not legal advice.

## 1) What this covers

"Bleverse Marks" include:

- **BLEVERSE** / **Bleverse** / **bleverse** (project name)
- Bleverse logos, icons, wordmarks, and brand visuals
- Any other names, slogans, or identifiers used to represent Bleverse as a project or product

Even if not registered, these identifiers may function as trademarks in practice.

## 2) Ownership

Bleverse Marks are owned and managed by:

- **Nazarii Korniienko**

Contributors retain copyright in their contributions (per the repository license). Trademark rights are separate from copyright.

## 3) Core principle

You may use Bleverse Marks only in ways that are:

- truthful and not misleading
- clear about what is official vs unofficial
- not implying endorsement or affiliation unless you have permission

Goal: **avoid user confusion.**

This policy exists to protect users and the integrity of the project — not to restrict legitimate open-source collaboration.

## 4) Allowed uses (no permission required)

### 4.1 Referring to Bleverse (nominative use)

You may use "Bleverse" to truthfully describe:

- the Bleverse project
- unmodified official releases
- compatibility statements (truthful and specific)

Examples:

- "Works with Bleverse."
- "A plugin for Bleverse."
- "Forked from Bleverse (unofficial)."

### 4.2 Tutorials, reviews, screenshots, community discussion

You may use the name "Bleverse" in educational or informational content (articles, videos, talks, documentation), as long as you do not present yourself as official.

### 4.3 Redistribution of unmodified official releases

You may redistribute unmodified official Bleverse releases with original branding intact, provided that you:

- preserve license notices and attributions
- do not claim to be the official project unless you are

## 5) Uses that require permission

Written permission is required to:

- use Bleverse logos or wordmarks in product UI or marketing materials
- name a company, product, or service in a way that appears official or endorsed
- operate a hosted service branded as "official Bleverse" (e.g., "Bleverse Cloud")
- produce or sell merchandise with Bleverse Marks
- register domains or social handles that appear official

Permission may include conditions (disclaimers, naming adjustments, etc.).

## 6) Prohibited uses

You may not:

- imply endorsement, sponsorship, or official status without permission
- use Bleverse logos for forks or modified distributions
- name a modified distribution primarily "Bleverse"
- use confusingly similar names or branding designed to appear official
- use Bleverse Marks for scams, malware, phishing, or unlawful activity

## 7) Forks and modified versions (naming rules)

If you fork or modify Bleverse and distribute it:

### 7.1 Choose a new primary name

Do **not** name your modified distribution "Bleverse".

Acceptable examples:

- "MyProject — a fork of Bleverse (unofficial)"
- "Based on Bleverse"
- "Bleverse-compatible plugin: MyPlugin"

Confusing examples:

- "Bleverse Pro"
- "Bleverse Enterprise"
- "Official Bleverse Fork"

### 7.2 Use your own branding

- Do not use Bleverse logos.
- Use your own name and visual identity.
- You may credit Bleverse in documentation.

### 7.3 Add a clear disclaimer (recommended)

> "This is an independent fork and is not affiliated with or endorsed by Bleverse."

## 8) Domains, social handles, and listings

Without permission, do not register or use:

- domains that appear official or are easily confused as official
- social handles suggesting official status
- app store listings presenting your product as "Bleverse" if it is not official

If referencing Bleverse in a domain or handle, independence must be clearly visible.

## 9) Packages and repositories

If you publish packages or plugins:

- Prefer names like `myorg-bleverse-plugin`, `plugin-for-bleverse`, `bleverse-compatible-*`
- Avoid names that resemble official core packages unless authorized

## 10) Brand assets

If this repository contains logo files or brand assets:

- They are provided for official Bleverse usage and community reference.
- Do not use them to brand forks or modified distributions.
- Do not modify them for use as your own branding.

## 11) Enforcement

If usage creates confusion or violates this policy, Bleverse may request reasonable changes (e.g., renaming a repository, removing logos, adjusting descriptions).

Misleading listings may be reported to relevant platforms when necessary.

## 12) Permission requests and reports

To request permission or report potential misuse, open a GitHub issue in this repository:

- **Trademark request:** describe intended usage, location, and whether it is commercial
- **Trademark report:** include links, screenshots, and explanation of the confusion

## 13) Updates

This policy may evolve over time. The latest version in the default branch is authoritative.
